This illustration depicts a traditional Thai house (บ้านเรือนไทย - Baan Ruen Thai), specifically a stilt house characteristic of old Siam (modern Thailand) As seen, the roof is steeply sloped, which helps with rainwater drainage, preventing leaks and damage.

Modular & Expandable Design – Traditional Thai homes were often built in sections, allowing them to be expanded or relocated if needed.

The painting shows open verandas, balconies, and interconnected walkways, which can encourage community interaction. As mentioned, these areas allow: Neighbors to easily interact and share resources. Families to gather for storytelling, crafting, or food preparation. A communal lifestyle where people live in harmony and help one another.

The windows are usually small and slanted outward, which provides shade and allows air circulation while keeping out rain. This means that the heat inside the house during hot days is reduced naturally, as well as limiting access view from outside.
