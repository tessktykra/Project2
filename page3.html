This image showcases different styles of traditional Thai dresses (ชุดไทย - Chut Thai), each with their own designs that reflect the purpose and historical time period. These dresses are often worn for formal events, royal ceremonies, and cultural occasions and reflect the many styles originated from the royal court.

Typically made from links of metal, the belt is usually fastened to hold the folds in the skirt in place, where the head of the belt can hook onto the chain links.

Paintings have referenced the different dresses and traditional outfit throughout Thai history, and in this one we can see a more casual type of dress, most likely from the North provinces in Thailand. A lot of these skirts and tops are a more casual version of the dresses we've seen, often for everday activities or dancing.

There have been many modern iterations of the traditional dress, often similar to Lisa's pictured here.
